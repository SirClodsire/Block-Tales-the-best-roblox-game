What even is Block Tales bro

Okok fine, for all of you guys who are too... ah, trivial about the games on Roblox,

(I'm goign to assume you guys all know what Roblox is), Block Tales is an story-based RPG

that is... set in the past? I don't want to spoil too much, but at the beginning, you are

transported back to the past, into the golden age of Roblox, which is around 2010.

 

Storytime! (Partial spoilers)

Now, I don't want to spoil too much of the plot, but essentially the story starts with you

watching the news. They announce that the Roblox VIP convention, which is an event where developers come together

and roblox announces the best game of their catagory for the year. For example, there could be a

best exploration game, best pvp game, best horror game even, ect. They host once every often, and it's a

friendly and fun gather around! Anyways, you exit your house and traverse modern Bizville, which is the town you live in,

and talk to the locals for a while before heading to the convention. There, you take your seat beside some penguins you met

earlier as the show starts.

 

David Bazuki, Aka Builderman, the ceo and leader of Roblox, stands up to give a speech. And not even two seconds this

poor man starts speaking, he imidiately gets jumped by this random dude wearing a dominus and his two cronies!

The man then starts rambling about how Builderman ruined roblox and his audacity to be standing here, talking to the

crowd about how amazing Roblox is at the moment. Builderman's helper decides to give-it a go hero and try to stop the man,

but the ball he throws was easily deflected by the man's two cronies, being a nooblet and a helmet noob (amazing selection, yes yes),

and he also gets absolutely bodied! Seeing this, the two young penguines you're sitting beside bursts into hyristicals, just in time as

their grandpa, and older penguin wakes up. He sees the mysterious man, and remarks how he remembers them before asking you to step up

and defeat him. Obviously, you're confused, especially when the old penguin insists that you've done this multiple times before, and

screams "I'LL KILL YOU!" before both of you guys step up!

 

And finally, this is the part where they teach you the combat system! I'm not going to say much, but it's very interesting to say the least.

it isn't fast-paced, 3-d and dynamic, but instead more of a mix of both skill and strategy. More on this later!

 

After you absolutely wreak these two nooblets, the dominus man exclaims how interesting this is. He said something about not remembering you

in this timeline, but is curious to see what will come next. He then raises a weird device over his head, and the old penguin screams for you to

destroy it, but you miss the throw, and you, along with builderman and the mysterious man, gets sucked in!

 

yeah im done with spoiling the story now TIME FOR WHAT MAKES THIS GAME GOOD!

 

So, why excatly is Block Tales your favorite game and what makes you think that I'LL ENJOY IT HUH?

First Impressions!

First of all, the second you log in, you already know it's not your avarage roblox game. While most these days rely on VFX and hype, block tales is unique

becuase it uses and almost 8-bit vibe that gives old players nostalgica and new players something new from the usual, so both audiences are

immidiately hooked. When you load in, the screen flashes BLOCK TALES: SWORDS OF TIME and a somewhat minimalistic old game style that sets it apart

from other loading screens already, and then you get transported to...

The main menu!

These days I feel like not a lot of games actually have main menus any more, a lot of them just throw you in, and as for the ones that do have main menus,

their designs are somewhat lacking in quality, or just not interesting. Block Tales, however, cover both interesting and well made. It's a somewhat minimalistic,

yet functional, fun and most importantly, unique menu.

It's not just the intro that leaves a good first impression though. The second you actually start playing, you can alread tell it's going to be a banger!

 

First of all, most roblox games are 3-D. I mean, I'm going to assume you guys all know what that means, but 3-D is short for 3 dimentional, which is how us humans

percive the world. And while it can be immersive, because you see it in pretty much all the games and even your day-to day life, it can get old. HOWEVER, Block Tales

brings this "immersiveness" to a whole new level because instead of boring old standard, they made their game not 3-D, not even 2-D, but something which

I like to call...

2.5-D!

And what is this 2.5-D, you ask? Good question, the answer is I STILL DON'T KNOW! It's hard to describe what this new perspective is until you actually play it!

but essentially, you know how 2-D games work, right? Most of you guys have played a fair few. Most platformers are 2-D, and so are some adventure games like

Hollow Knight and Celeste! (absolute banger games!) But in Block tales, it's a 3-d invironement in a 2-D perspective...

The Story!

Like I mentioned earlier, Block Tales is a story based RPG, which means there is a story you follow in this game, and as the story progresses, so does your character.

However, to get the player to progress and continue playing, all story based RPGs needs a captivating story to grab the attention of the player

and make sure they continue playing, and Block Tales doesn't dissapoint! The story is extremely well crafted, and the entire time as I was playing it, I was always

keen to know what happens next. It's extremely funny at times, and also crippling sad at others. It really makes you feel a variety of emotions as you play it, ok?

at times, this game has GENUENELY made me sad. Some of your actions are finite in this game, which means you can't really undo some of the mistakes you make, and it

can be hard to get certain characters back on your side after you've pissed them off. Something else I really like is that the devs really put in effort to add

dialogue to some npc's that the player might not even find! There are definitly multiple easter eggs that even I still haven't found yet.
